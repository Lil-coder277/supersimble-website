  • Customizable vehicles with cool designs
  • Exciting tournaments and events

Ranking System

Rank Division Requirements
Super Sonic Legend I Top players globally, extremely rare. This rank is for extremely talented players globally; it takes skill to get here. A lot of YouTubers are at this rank.
Grand Champion II Top 100 players in the world. This is a very hard rank to reach; people who are here have tens of thousands of hours put into the game.
Champion I Top 0.1% of players. These are the players that grind for hours, training themselves, born with the skill set for the game.
Diamond III Top 1% of players. In this rank, players get very skilled, and the play style is very challenging.
Platinum IV Top 5% of players. In this rank, you basically combine the skills you know as you progress into the ranks and teach yourself new skill sets because you could be stuck in that rank for a while.
Gold II Top 25% of players. Gold players start learning advanced mechanics, including aerial plays and effective rotation. This rank marks a significant skill improvement compared to the lower ranks.
Silver I Beginner/Intermediate players. This is where the game starts getting serious, knowing the rules and strategies. Silver players focus on fundamental skills, ball control, and basic positioning.
Bronze V Transitioning to the next skill level. Bronze V players refine their overall gameplay, emphasizing teamwork and adapting to various in-game situations. This rank sets the stage for climbing higher in the ranks.
Bronze IV Further development for novice players. Bronze IV players work on incorporating more advanced mechanics into their gameplay, including basic aerial maneuvers and positioning strategies.
Bronze III Progressing through the novice stage. Bronze III players aim to enhance their game awareness and decision-making, focusing on efficient rotations and adapting to different playstyles.
Bronze II Continued learning for novice players. Bronze II players work on refining basic skills, such as ball control and positioning, as they gain a better understanding of game dynamics.
Bronze I Novice players, learning the game. At this rank, players focus on understanding basic mechanics and game concepts, building a foundation for future improvement.
